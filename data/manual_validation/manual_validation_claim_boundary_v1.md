# Manual Validation Claim Boundary v1

## Completed validation inputs

The project received three manually annotated validation files under `hssc深圳-标注完毕/`. They were read as GB18030 and standardised to UTF-8 derived files under `data_processed/validation/`.

## Main empirical update

- The traceable 132-record rule-source ledger is strongly supported: 127 of 132 records passed manual verification (96.2%).
- The 300-record priority rule-liminal queue converts into source-verifiable evidence much less often: 38 of 300 records passed (12.7%).
- The 1,000-record stratified rule-liminal queue shows the same pattern at larger scale: 77 of 1,000 records passed (7.7%).

## Correct interpretation

Manual validation supports a dual structure. The curated rule-source ledger is reliable enough to anchor the visible-rule layer. By contrast, the much larger rule-liminal host layer should not be interpreted as a hidden reservoir of already pet-friendly venues. It is better read as a structured field of rule non-publicity: places where access is plausible or institutionally imaginable but usually not verifiable from public or operator-facing sources.

## Claim boundary

- `validation_decision = 通过` means the evidence or rule direction was source-verifiable in manual review.
- It does not mean that the venue is open to companion animals.
- Passed rows include restrictive, conditional, open/designated, governance-support and ambiguous cases.
- Failed rows should not be recoded as closed venues. They are non-verifiable within the current public/operator-facing search protocol.
- Rule-liminal hosts remain candidate or diagnostic evidence unless a specific source-verifiable access direction is attached.

## Manuscript consequence

The strongest revised claim is not that many hidden venues are pet-friendly. The stronger and safer claim is that Shenzhen contains a large material and venue ecology in which pet access remains weakly publicised. The pet-friendly city is therefore suspended between service abundance and rule legibility.
